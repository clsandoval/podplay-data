# KAN-16: FC Porto Academy

## Description

FC Porto Academy is FC Porto’s youth program. It helps young players grow with a European approach: strong technique, smart tactics, solid fitness, and good character.

## Comments

### Richard Bachmann — 2026-05-11

From my understanding, the current proposal is to create a NEW company structure where we would hold 70% ownership while FA Manila retains 30%. Conceptually, I understand the value of using an existing club platform given the new NCR league structure and the difficulty of building a club from scratch today. However, the actual mechanics and structure of the deal are still not fully clear to us yet.Before we move forward further, I think we need to get alignment and clarity on several areas:- Ownership & Deal Structure
- Is this essentially a share swap structure where the existing FA Manila stakeholders receive 30% ownership in the NEW company without a direct monetary buyout?


- Who exactly will own the remaining 30% on the FA Manila side?


- What exactly are we acquiring or gaining aside from the existing player/member base?- League participation rights?


- Existing brand equity?


- Operational infrastructure?


- Coaching system?


- Existing sponsor relationships?


- Will the existing FA Manila entity continue operating separately, or will everything transition into the NEW company structure?
- Shareholders & Corporate Information




We also need a clearer understanding of the current ownership and legal structure of FA Manila.
- Mico mentioned Sergie and two other parents currently own FA Manila. Are there additional shareholders beyond those mentioned?


- Since FA Manila is SEC registered, we should probably request the following as part of due diligence:- Latest GIS


- Latest AFS


- Cap table / ownership structure


- Banking information


- Existing liabilities or obligations if any
- Operations & Personnel




We should also clarify operational expectations early.
- Are we expected to retain:- Existing coaching staff?


- The current Spanish Technical Director?


- Existing admin personnel?


- Are any of these contractual obligations or simply preferred arrangements?


- I also think it would be good for us to interview the admin team and selected coaches to better understand how the club currently operates.
- League & Transition Questions




Need clarity as well on how the transition works operationally with the league.
- If the club eventually rebrands into Dragonforce FA Manila during an active season, will this create any league issues or restrictions?


- For players who become interested in transferring after participating in activities like the World Camp, my understanding is they would simply need to wait until the season ends before formally changing clubs. We should confirm this.
- Membership Retention & Business Risk




This is probably one of the biggest business risks.




A large part of the value here is tied to the existing membership base. If after the transition a meaningful percentage of players leave, we need to understand:
- How that risk is viewed by both sides


- Whether there are retention strategies already in place


- How operational and financial responsibilities would be handled during the 2-3 year build period being proposed
Overall, I do agree there is strategic value in acquiring an existing operational platform instead of starting from zero, especially under the evolving NCR system. But before committing capital and resources, I think we need full clarity on:- what exactly we are acquiring,


- what obligations come with it,


- what operational flexibility we will have,


- and how risk is shared between both parties.

- If the club eventually rebrands into Dragonforce FA Manila during an active season, will this create any league issues or restrictions?- Only academy name will change so there will be no restrictions because it is only a rebranding of the current team.

- Under the new youth league rules, once the league starts, any player who decides to transfer clubs will be disqualified from continuing to play in the league for that season. They will have to wait for the next season to be able to play in the league with the club they transferred to.
So my suggestion is, we do the World Camp towards the end of the season. The waiting period of those interested to move to Dragonforce will be short. If the league will end in May 2027, in my opinion for timing purposes, we should do the world camp earliest Feb 2027 or March 2027.
